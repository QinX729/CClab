In unknown coastal waters on July 29th, 2000, by Dr. Sinc.

Family:

✨🐟Spectrish🐟✨ usually move around in a group of three. They got their name from their ability to shift colors through a beautiful spectrum between two set hues. Most of the time, ✨🐟Spectrish🐟✨ within the same group share the same color spectrum, though each individual varies slightly in movement and the pace of color transition. Their synchronized glow in the ocean often makes them appear like living rainbows beneath the waves.

Appearance:

At first sight, ✨🐟Spectrish🐟✨ appear quite simple. Their heads are nearly perfect spheres, with elegant, silk-like tails gently waving behind their bodies. Under the light, these tails shimmer as if woven from liquid light. Their simple form and psychedelic colors make them true little geniuses of the sea.

Habitat:

✨🐟Spectrish🐟✨ are remarkably friendly creatures. They are naturally curious about humans and will willingly approach anyone who enters their detectable range. Various observations have shown that ✨🐟Spectrish🐟✨'s color-changing behavior is linked to the weather. Multiple researchers, fishermen, and tourists have reported that ✨🐟Spectrish🐟✨ tend to shift colors more rapidly during storms. In contrast, on sunny and warm days when the tides are calm, their colors flow softly, reflecting the peace of the sea.

Key Characteristics:

  • Always apear in groups of three.
  • Continuously change colors.
  • Shift colors more rapidly when the weather turns stormy.
  • Friendly and curious towards human beings.
